Multiple Intelligences is a theory proposed by psychologist Howard Gardner in 1983. It suggests that intelligence is not a single, general ability but rather a variety of distinct types of intelligences that individuals possess in varying degrees. According to Gardner, people have different ways of processing information and learning, and traditional IQ tests don't capture the full range of human potential.

“Since February 2024, Palawan Bethel Senior High School has been championing the Multiple Intelligences Initiative, recognizing that every student has unique strengths and potential. From linguistic and logical-mathematical skills to musical and bodily-kinesthetic talents, we are committed to nurturing all areas of intelligence, allowing each student to thrive in their own way. We believe that education should be personalized, empowering students to discover and develop their natural gifts.”
